The National Association of Realtors (NAR) Settlement Shakeup

Imported from the live site: rocketstation.com/the-national-association-of-realtors-or-nar-settlement-shakeup/

This guide explains how the NAR settlement reshapes real estate operations and highlights the growing importance of virtual assistants in navigating industry changes.

Key Changes in the NAR Settlement

The settlement, effective August 17, 2024, fundamentally restructures buyer-agent compensation. The core change — the elimination of compensation offers in MLS listings — shifts the industry from transparent commission displays to direct negotiation between buyers and their agents.

Impact on Real Estate Professionals

For agents: the settlement requires written agreements with buyers before home showings, creating substantial new administrative burdens. Agents must now justify fees individually rather than relying on standard MLS-listed commissions.

For brokers: leadership must revise compensation models, update training programs, and enhance transparency throughout their operations.

For investors: changes may enable lower transaction costs due to more competitive agent fees, though investors must conduct more thorough vetting of agent services.

Virtual Assistants as a Strategic Solution

Virtual assistants offer essential support for managing increased documentation, handling client communications about fees, tracking compliance measures, and coordinating transactions — allowing agents to focus on relationship-building and closing deals amid regulatory complexity.
